Sentences

Traditional class logic uses four forms of sentences which are traditionally labeled A, E, I and O.

A      All __________ are __________.

E      No __________ are __________.

I      Some __________ are __________.

O      Some __________ are not __________.

The blanks are filled in with different class terms to generate sentences. For example, "All Siberian huskies are stubborn dogs" is a sentence of the A-form which expresses a relation between Siberian huskies and stubborn dogs. The blanks may also be filled in with the terms for the complements of classes, e.g., "Some non-amphibians are not non-mammals".

Immediate Inferences

Sentences can be combined to produce arguments in which some sentences, the premises, are taken to support another sentence, the conclusion. An immediate inference is an argument which has only one premise and a conclusion each in the form A, E, I or O. For example, the following is an immediate inference.

No plants are animals. (premise)
----------------------
No animals are plants. (conclusion)

This is a good argument in that it is valid. An argument is valid if and only if it is not possible for its premise(s) to be true and its conclusion false. Here is an invalid argument:

Some animals are dogs. (premise)
----------------------
All animals are dogs. (conclusion)

Notice that in this case it is possible for the premise to be true and the conclusion false, therefore this argument is invalid.

Syllogisms

A special form of argument, the syllogism, plays a prominent role in traditional class logic. Here is an example of a syllogism:

All reptiles are animals. (premise)
Some snakes are reptiles. (premise)
-------------------------
Some snakes are animals. (conclusion)

A syllogism is an argument that has two premises and a conclusion each in the form A, E, I or O and that has exactly three class terms: a major term (predicate of conclusion and occurs in first premise), a minor term (subject of conclusion and occurs in second premise) and a middle term (occurs in both premises).
